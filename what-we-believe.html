What We Believe

A concise summary of our convictions, rooted in Scripture and pursued with logic, care, and context.

Core Summary

We confess the authority and sufficiency of the Holy Scriptures, the triune God, the person and work of Jesus the Messiah, salvation by grace through faith, and a life of obedience empowered by the Spirit.

  • God has spoken clearly in Scripture and calls us to “reason together” (Isa 1:18) and “test everything” (1 Th 5:21).
  • Logic serves love and clarity; Scripture interprets Scripture.
  • We pursue biblical faithfulness with humility, joy, and courage.

Scripture

The Scriptures are God-breathed, truthful, and authoritative for faith and life (2 Tim 3:16–17; Jn 17:17). We submit our minds and methods to the Word of God.

  • Clarity: God communicates to be understood (Neh 8:8; Ps 119).
  • Consistency: The sum of God’s Word is truth (Ps 119:160).
  • Christ-centered: The Law and the Prophets point to the Messiah (Lk 24:27).

God

There is one God, eternal, holy, and faithful (Deut 6:4; Mal 3:6). He is the Creator and Sustainer of all things and reveals Himself truly in Scripture.

Humanity

Humanity is created in God’s image with dignity and purpose (Gen 1:26–27), yet fallen in sin (Rom 3:23). Our hope is found in God’s redeeming grace.

Salvation

Salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus the Messiah (Eph 2:8–9). He lived, died, and rose again to save sinners and reconcile us to God.

Church

Christ gathers His people to worship, grow, and witness (Acts 2:42–47). We devote ourselves to Scripture, prayer, fellowship, and faithful mission.

Future Hope

Jesus will return; God will judge righteously; and He will make all things new (Rev 21–22). Therefore, we live with watchful hope and steadfast love.
