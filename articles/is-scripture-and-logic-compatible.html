Is Scripture & Logic Compatible?

Most people approach the Holy Bible for many different reasons: for answers to life's questions, for academic reasons, or some other reason not mentioned here. No matter the purpose of approaching the text, the Bible demands we approach it carefully and learn to consistently read its pages through the lens of logic. God calls His people to “reason together” (Isaiah 1:18), to “test everything; hold fast what is good” (1 Thessalonians 5:21), and to examine the Scriptures to see if things are so (Acts 17:11). Logic is not an opponent of our faith but a humble servant of truth.

Logic Serves Love and Clarity

For example, since God is not the author of confusion (1 Corinthians 14:33), God commands we pursue clarity (Gk, agape; brotherly love) in all things. The Holy Spirit commanded Paul to urge us to have repeated thoughtful reflection: “Think over what I say, for the Lord will give you understanding” (2 Timothy 2:7). Jesus, The Son of God, Himself reasoned from the Law and the Prophets to show how they point to Him (Luke 24:27).

Scripture Interprets Scripture

Sound interpretation follows Scripture’s own patterns, this is the only biblical hermeneutic (interpretation). The Bereans (Israelite group) tested the apostles’ claims with the text (Acts 17:11), and Paul reasoned from the Scriptures as his standard (Acts 17:2). Wisdom begins with reverence—“Get wisdom” (Proverbs 4:7)—and reverence can never be irrational. It orders the mind and heart under God’s Word.

Logic does not add to Scripture; it helps us trace the lines God already drew.

When logic serves our love of God and our neighbor, it becomes an amazing tool of worship to God. We think, test, and reason so that we might believe, obey, and rejoice in the truth.
