Is Truth Reliable?

Semitic Jew • Article

This article addresses a simple question: Is truth reliable? We answer with three claims rooted in Scripture and reason.

A. God is Truth — and everything from Him and His people is truthful

God is not merely a teller of truth; He is the source of it. What proceeds from Him—His word, commandments, covenants—and what proceeds from His covenant people when faithful to Him participates in that truth. Reliability follows from God’s unchanging nature (Jn 17:17).

B. The devil is the father of lies — and what proceeds from him and his people is deception

Scripture names deception as moral rebellion. Falsehood distorts what God has made plain, mixing fragments of truth to mislead (Jn 8:44). Discernment tests claims by Scripture and consistent reasoning.

C. The world has been manipulated by severing Israel’s identity from history — our work is to restore it

A grave deception is historical: the enemy has labored to divorce the reality of the Israelites—Black, Afro‑Semitic peoples—from the annals of public memory. Our task is to gather primary texts, material culture, and historical witnesses, and to reason soundly, so that the dignity of God’s ancient people is restored to public knowledge (Isa 59:14).

Truth is reliable because God is reliable; deception is exposed because Scripture unveils it; research repairs what lies have broken.
