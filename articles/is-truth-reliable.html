Is Truth Reliable?

Semitic Jew • Article

This article addresses a simple question: Is truth reliable? We answer with three claims rooted in Scripture and reason. The discussion is intentionally extended for readers who want the logic laid out step by step.

God is Truth — the Source through Logic, Nature, and Rev

God is not merely a teller of truth; He is the source of it. What proceeds from Him—His word, commandments, and covenants—and what proceeds from His covenant people when faithful to Him participates in that truth (Jn 17:17, Ps 119:160).

1) Logic reflects the God who is consistent

The laws of logic—identity, non‑contradiction, and excluded middle—mirror the faithfulness of God who does not deny Himself. Because God is one and truthful, contradictions cannot be true of Him or His word.

2) Nature bears witness to God’s truth

Creation communicates knowledge about its Maker: design, order, and sustenance point beyond themselves (Ps 19:1–4, Rom 1:20, Isa 40:26). The stability of nature’s patterns underwrites honest inquiry and dependable testimony.

3) Scripture reveals and interprets

Special revelation—God speaking in Scripture—grounds doctrine and judges our conclusions (II Tim 3:16–17). Where human reasoning errs, Scripture corrects; where observation is partial, Scripture supplies the larger frame.

Satan is A Deceiver — distorting truth with fallacies and false witness

Scripture names deception as moral rebellion. The adversary’s strategy from the beginning is to twist God’s words, to sow doubt, and to replace sound reasoning with sophistry (Gen 3:1, Jn 8:44). His servants masquerade as righteous, trading in persuasive but empty speech (II Cor 11:13–15).

1) Common fallacies weaponized

  • Stra​​w man: misrepresenting Scripture or a position to knock down a caricature (Matt 4:6–7).
  • Appeal to majority/authority: elevating consensus or prestige above revealed truth (Jn 12:43).
  • Equivocation: shifting meanings mid‑argument to confuse judgments (II Peter 3:16).
  • Ad hominem: attacking the witness instead of testing the claim (Acts 24:5).

2) False research & misused sources

Deception flourishes when data is cherry‑picked, citations are de‑contextualized, and methods are bent toward foregone conclusions (Eph 4:14). Sound method demands transparent sources, consistent standards of evidence, and willingness to be corrected by the text.

3) Misrepresenting Scripture

When Scripture is fragmented from its context, doctrines are distorted. The antidote is to let the canonical voice harmonize with itself, line upon line and precept upon precept (Isa 28:10).

Satan Deceives The Earth — nations as instruments of propaganda and concealment

The dragon’s reach is global: he deceives the whole world (Rev 12:9, Rev 20:3, Rev 20:8), and earthly powers often serve that deception through propaganda, unjust policies, and suppression of testimony (Isa 5:20, Mic 3:9–11).

1) Tools of mass deception

  • Propaganda: repeated narratives that invert moral terms and rewrite history (Dan 8:12).
  • False teaching: doctrines crafted to please the audience rather than honor God (II Timothy 4:3–4).
  • Gatekeeping knowledge: restricting access to sources and punishing dissent (Luke 11:52).

2) Concealing Israel’s identity

Among the gravest distortions is the effort to sever public memory from the biblical and historical reality of Israel as a Black, Afro‑Semitic people. God permits testing; Satan exploits it to obscure testimony. Our response is painstaking research: assembling primary texts, material culture, linguistic evidence, and consistent argument—so that truth and dignity are restored to view (Jer 6:16, Rev 18:23).

Conclusion — Why truth remains reliable

Truth is reliable because God is reliable. Deception can be identified because Scripture exposes it and logic unmasks its contradictions. Therefore the people of God must read carefully, reason consistently, check sources, and bear witness—even when nations prefer a flattering lie to a humbling truth.
